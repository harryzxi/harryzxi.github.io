Phase-Type Distributions for Problems in Delivery Management

Description:

In representing relationships between entities, graphs provide a useful mathematical abstraction to the analysis of such relationships through "social networks". In the language of graph theory, cliques refer to complete subgraphs, and correspond to communities within the context of social networks. We combine efficient computational methods such as greedy colouring and vertex ordering with theorems from combinatorics (coloured complexes) to produce procedures to bound the number of k-cliques within large real-world social networks via experiments.
